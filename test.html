"In the world of computing, Moore's Law has been a fundamental principle since its formulation by Gordon Moore, co-founder of Intel, in 1965. This law states that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles approximately every two years, while the cost of these chips decreases. This extraordinary rate of advancement in semiconductor technology has been the driving force behind the exponential growth in computing power, making today's computers exponentially more powerful and efficient compared to their predecessors from just a few years ago. Moore's Law has played a pivotal role in shaping the modern digital landscape, influencing everything from personal computers and smartphones to the development of cutting-edge technologies like artificial intelligence and quantum computing."
